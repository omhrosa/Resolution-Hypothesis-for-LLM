# Resolution Hypothesis

A conceptual framework for understanding capability emergence in AI systems through increasing representational fidelity ("resolution").

## Core Idea

Human training corpora contain:

```text
Knowledge
+
Cognitive Patterns
```

As AI systems improve, they become increasingly capable of faithfully representing both.

The hypothesis proposes that many advanced capabilities commonly treated as separate faculties—reasoning, abstraction, planning, analogy, generalization, and contextual understanding—may instead be manifestations of a common underlying phenomenon:

**Increasing Resolution.**

## High-Level Mechanism

```text
Architecture
+
Attention
+
Training
+
Data
+
Scale
↓
Resolution
↓
More Faithful Representation
of Knowledge and Cognitive Patterns
↓
More Sophisticated Behavior
```

## Key Claim

The hypothesis does not view scale as the fundamental variable.

Scale is one mechanism that can increase resolution.

Resolution is the proposed underlying variable.

Future architectures may achieve similar or higher resolution through entirely different methods.

## Repository Purpose

This repository is an open collection of ideas, discussion, predictions, and falsifiable claims related to the Resolution Hypothesis.

It is not presented as a proven theory.

The goal is to provide a framework that can be criticized, refined, tested, or extended.
